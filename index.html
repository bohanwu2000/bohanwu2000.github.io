I am a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics at Columbia University, advised by Professor David Blei.

My research lies at the foundations of probabilistic machine learning. I develop mathematically rigorous and computationally scalable methods for probabilistic inference, with a focus on variational inference, empirical Bayes, optimal transport, and causal modeling.

I am interested in understanding and improving the statistical and computational properties of modern inference algorithms, especially those that are widely used in machine learning but lack firm theoretical guarantees.

My research interests include:

  • Variational inference and Bayesian computation: stability, mode collapse, structured variational families, and distributionally robust inference
  • Empirical Bayes and simultaneous inference: probabilistic symmetries, representation learning, causal inference, and shrinkage methods
  • Optimal transport and geometry of learning: Wasserstein methods for inference, sampling, robustness, and generative modeling
  • Applications: genomics, biomedical data analysis, causal discovery, and scientific machine learning
